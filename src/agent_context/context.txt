Search Query: Duan Yongping biography early career BBK Electronics and transition to investing
Search Answer: Duan Yongping is a Chinese-American billionaire entrepreneur born in Nanchang, China, in 1961 [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duan_Yongping]. He attended Zhejiang University in 1978, where he majored in wireless electronics engineering [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duan_Yongping, https://kr-asia.com/duan-yongping-from-chopping-firewood-in-the-countryside-to-creating-a-billion-dollar-legacy-part-1-of-3]. His early career began at a state-run vacuum tube factory and later at Zhongshan Yihua Group in 1989 [https://grokipedia.com/page/Duan_Yongping]. He also served as the director of the Subor Electronics Industry Corporation, which became a top producer of "learning computers" under his leadership [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duan_Yongping].

In 1995, Duan resigned from Subor and founded BBK Electronics Group in Guangdong Province [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duan_Yongping]. BBK is the parent company for smartphone brands like OPPO, Vivo, OnePlus, and Realme, which collectively became the largest phone manufacturer in the world [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIggp85vgZA].

To manage his finances, Duan Yongping established an investment firm called H&H International Investment [https://www.granitefirm.com/blog/us/2024/01/15/duan-yongping/]. One of his most successful investments was in NetEase, which yielded a return of nearly 100 times its original value [https://www.granitefirm.com/blog/us/2024/01/15/duan-yongping/]. It is reported that he has invested in no more than six major companies [https://www.granitefirm.com/blog/us/2024/01/15/duan-yongping/].

Search Query: Warren Buffett investment philosophy influence from Benjamin Graham and Philip Fisher

Graham is known as the father of value investing [https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/warren-buffett-art-science-investing-sara-volpato-udnuf], and Buffett has shared his experiences working with him [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SW-JNv8nCYk]. One analysis suggests Buffett's current approach may align more with Fisher than Graham, but notes that both men were contrarian investors [https://www.jvbruni.com/articles/the-philosophy-of-phillip-fisher].

Search Query: Charlie Munger biography early career pre-Berkshire investment partnership intellectual influences
Search Answer: Before becoming the Vice Chairman of Berkshire Hathaway alongside Warren Buffett, Charlie Munger was trained as a meteorologist during World War II and later as a lawyer at Harvard [https://fs.blog/intellectual-giants/charlie-munger/, https://commoncog.com/c/cases/charlie-munger-first-million/]. In 1945, he married Nancy Huggins [https://commoncog.com/c/cases/charlie-munger-first-million/]. His early business dealings included performing legal work for an entrepreneur named Ed Hoskins and his company, Transformer Engineers Ltd., around 1950. Munger later borrowed money to buy into the business in what he described as an early leveraged buyout [https://commoncog.com/c/cases/charlie-munger-first-million/]. As he developed his investment skills, his investments began yielding annual returns of over 20%, which was four times the market average at the time [https://keshbagri.medium.com/the-amazing-life-story-of-charlie-munger-before-berkshire-hathaway-2505d885544b]. Munger was intellectually influenced by Warren Buffett and Benjamin Graham, adopting value investing as a core principle [https://quartr.com/insights/edge/the-extraordinary-life-of-charlie-munger]. He also drew insights from psychology and was known for his use of mental models and considering the psychological aspects of investing [https://fs.blog/intellectual-giants/charlie-munger/, https://quartr.com/insights/edge/the-extraordinary-life-of-charlie-munger].

Search Query: Warren Buffett Charlie Munger core investment tenets "Circle of Competence" "Economic Moats" "Margin of Safety"

Search Query: Warren Buffett Charlie Munger influence shift from Graham's "fair companies at wonderful prices" to "wonderful companies at fair prices"
Search Answer: Charlie Munger was instrumental in shifting Warren Buffett's investment philosophy away from the teachings of Ben Graham [https://x.com/InvestingCanons/status/1990069191601160295]. The core of this change was moving from buying "a fair company at a wonderful price" to buying "a wonderful company at a fair price" [https://indianexpress.com/article/smart-stocks/charlie-munger-changed-warren-buffett-investors-10135234/, https://www.foxbusiness.com/business-leaders/warren-buffett-charlie-mungers-surprise-no-1-investment]. Instead of purchasing distressed or the cheapest available businesses, Munger persuaded Buffett to focus on acquiring high-quality companies at fair, or even seemingly high, prices [https://www.inc.com/jeff-haden/the-780-billion-man-how-charlie-mungers-blueprint-changed-warren-buffetts-approach-to-investing.html, https://ca.finance.yahoo.com/news/5-investing-lessons-learned-charlie-110059649.html]. This focus on quality became a central element of their shared investment thesis [https://www.foxbusiness.com/business-leaders/warren-buffett-charlie-mungers-surprise-no-1-investment].
